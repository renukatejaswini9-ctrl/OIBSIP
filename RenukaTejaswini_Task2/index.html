Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian aerospace scientist, teacher, author, and the 11th President of India. He played a major role in India's missile and nuclear programs, earning the title "Missile Man of India."

Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam came from a humble background and rose to become one of India's most respected scientists and leaders. His dedication to education and innovation inspired millions of students across the country.

He received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award. Even after serving as President, he continued motivating young minds through lectures and books.

Major Achievements

  • Known as the Missile Man of India
  • Led India's Agni and Prithvi missile projects
  • Played a key role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007)
  • Received the Bharat Ratna Award
  • Inspired millions of students through education
  • Authored famous books such as Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds
